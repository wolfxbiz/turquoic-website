One of the most common questions we get from ecommerce business owners in Kerala is: **"Should I use Shopify or build a custom website?"**

There's no universal answer — it depends on your stage, budget, and long-term goals. This guide breaks it down honestly.

## Shopify: The Quick Start Option

Shopify is a hosted ecommerce platform that handles the technical infrastructure for you — hosting, security, payment processing, and software updates.

**Best for:** Early-stage D2C brands in Kerala with under ₹50L annual revenue who want to launch fast and test their market.

### Shopify Pros
- **Launch in days** — templates, drag-and-drop, no coding needed
- **Reliable hosting** — 99.9% uptime, auto-scaling
- **Built-in payment gateway** — Shopify Payments or Razorpay integration
- **App ecosystem** — thousands of plugins for reviews, upsells, loyalty programs
- **Lower upfront cost** — ₹2,000–₹7,500/month plans vs ₹80,000+ custom build

### Shopify Cons
- **Monthly fees forever** — you're renting, not owning
- **Limited SEO control** — URL structures, technical customization are restricted
- **Transaction fees** — Shopify charges 0.5–2% per transaction unless you use Shopify Payments
- **Looks like a Shopify store** — hard to stand out from competitors using the same templates
- **Customization ceiling** — complex features require expensive developers or app fees

## Custom Website: The Long-Term Asset

A custom-built ecommerce website (typically on Next.js, or a fully custom WordPress/WooCommerce setup) gives you complete control over every aspect of your store.

**Best for:** Established Kerala brands with consistent revenue, complex product catalogs, or strong SEO ambitions.

### Custom Website Pros
- **Full SEO control** — custom URL structures, server-side rendering, schema markup, lightning-fast page loads
- **No monthly platform fees** — pay once, own forever (only hosting costs)
- **Unlimited customization** — build exactly what your customers need
- **Better performance** — Next.js ecommerce stores regularly score 95+ on PageSpeed vs 50–70 for Shopify
- **No transaction fees**

### Custom Website Cons
- **Higher upfront cost** — ₹80,000 to ₹2,50,000+ depending on scope
- **Longer build time** — 4–10 weeks vs days for Shopify
- **Requires a development partner** for ongoing changes and maintenance
- **You're responsible for security and updates**

## The Kerala Ecommerce Context

Kerala's ecommerce market has specific characteristics worth considering:

**Mobile-first audience** — 80%+ of Kerala shoppers browse on mobile. Both Shopify and custom sites can be mobile-optimized, but custom sites built on Next.js typically outperform Shopify on mobile Core Web Vitals.

**Regional language expectations** — Many Kerala customers prefer Malayalam content. Custom sites make bilingual support much easier.

**Local payment preferences** — UPI, Net Banking, and COD are heavily used. Both platforms support Razorpay which covers all of these.

**Competitive landscape** — If you're competing with other Kerala brands on Google Shopping, SEO performance matters enormously. Custom Next.js sites have a measurable advantage here.

## Our Recommendation: By Stage

| Business Stage | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Just starting, < ₹10L revenue | Shopify — validate before you invest |
| Growing, ₹10L–₹1Cr revenue | Shopify with serious SEO investment OR custom |
| Scaling, ₹1Cr+ revenue | Custom website — SEO and performance ROI justifies the cost |
| Complex catalog or B2B | Custom — Shopify's limitations will block you |

## The Hybrid Approach

Some Kerala brands use **Shopify for transactions** and a **custom Next.js frontend** (called headless commerce). This gives you Shopify's reliability plus full frontend performance control.

It's more expensive and complex, but it's what fast-growing D2C brands eventually move to.
